Restoration: Care During Illness

Restoration focuses on returning the child to optimal health after acute illness, managing chronic conditions, and minimizing complications. Key components:

  • Physical restoration: Administer medications, monitor vital signs, promote hydration/nutrition, manage symptoms (pain, fever, dyspnea).
  • Emotional restoration: Address fear, anxiety, regression; provide age‑appropriate explanations and play therapy.
  • Family support: Involve parents in care, offer education about illness and home care, connect to resources.
Key principle: Child’s developmental stage influences how they perceive illness – use therapeutic communication and play.

Impact of Hospitalization

Infant/Toddler
Separation anxiety (protest, despair, detachment), stranger anxiety, regression (thumbsucking, bedwetting).
Preschool
Fear of bodily harm/mutilation, magical thinking, guilt (believes illness is punishment), regression.
School‑Age & Adolescent
Loss of control, fear of death, body image changes, separation from peers, loss of privacy.

Nursing Interventions & Family‑Centered Care

  • Prepare child for procedures: Use age‑appropriate language, play therapy, allow choices (when possible).
  • Minimize separation: Encourage rooming‑in, flexible visiting hours, parental presence during procedures.
  • Maintain routines: Follow home sleep/meal schedules, bring familiar objects (blanket, toy).
  • Provide play and education: Therapeutic play to express feelings, school re‑entry programs.
  • Support siblings: Explain illness, prevent jealousy, arrange visits.
Mnemonic "P.A.L.S.": Prepare, Allow choices, Limit separation, Support routines.

Health Maintenance (Well‑Child)

Well‑child care includes regular health supervision visits for growth monitoring, immunizations, developmental screening, injury prevention, nutrition counseling, and anticipatory guidance.

📏 Growth Monitoring
Plot height, weight, head circumference on standardized growth charts. Identify faltering growth or obesity.
💉 Immunizations
Follow national immunization schedule (CDC/Philippines EPI). Key vaccines: HepB, DTaP, IPV, Hib, PCV, MMR, Varicella, HPV, Tdap, Meningococcal.
🧩 Developmental Surveillance
Use tools like Denver II, PEDS. Screen for autism (M‑CHAT at 18 & 24 months).
🍎 Nutrition & Oral Health
Breastfeeding support, introduction of solids, limit sugar, dental visits by age 1, fluoride.

Chronic Illness & Special Needs

  • Impact on child: Limited activities, social isolation, school absences, altered body image, depression.
  • Impact on family: Financial strain, caregiver fatigue, marital stress, neglect of siblings, grief.
  • Nursing role: Coordinate care, promote normalcy, support self‑management, connect to community resources (support groups, respite care).
  • Transition to adult care: Start early, teach self‑advocacy, ensure continuity.
Mnemonic "C.O.P.E.": Coordinate services, Offer emotional support, Promote independence, Educate family.
